\item{1.} (NZM, Problam 1.3.27) Show that if $n$ is {\it not} prime, then $n|(n-1)!$ .

\item{2.} (NZM, Problam 1.3.32) Show that for $n>1$, $n^4+4$ is {\it never} prime.

\item{}(Hint: $f(x)= x^4+4$ can be expressed as a product of quadratics; find the factorization!)

\item{3.} (NZM, Problem 2.1.27) Show, by induction, that 
$\displaystyle{{1}\over{5}}n^5+{{1}\over{3}}n^3+{{7}\over{15}}n$ is an integer for every $n\geq 1$ .
(Note, however, that it is {\it not} a multiple of $n$ !)

\item{4.} Show, by induction on $n$ that 

\ssk

\ssk

\item{}(Hint: prove the statement in brackets [$\cdots$] by induction on $x$ !)

\bsk

\item{5.} Show that if $a\geq 2$ and $a^n-1$ is prime, then $n$ must be prime.
