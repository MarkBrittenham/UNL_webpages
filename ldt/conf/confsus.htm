LOW DIMENSIONAL TOPOLOGY CONFERENCE

Universities of Sussex and London

England

April 11-14, 14-17, 1997

Theme of Conference: Knots, 3-manifolds and other LOW DIMENSIONAL TOPOLOGY phenomena.

Tentative list of speakers: M.Boileau (Toulouse), G.Burde (Frankfurt), C.Gordon (Austin), N.Habegger (Nantes), J.Hoste (Pitzer), J.Howie (Heriot-Watt), Y.Mathieu (Provence), Y.Matsumoto (Tokyo), J.Montesinos (Madrid), Y.Moriah (Technion), D.Rolfsen (UBC), C.Rourke (Warwick), J.Simon (Iowa), D.Sumners (Florida).

Venues and Dates:

  1. University of Sussex Isle of Thorns Conference Centre at Chelwood Gate. Friday 11 April-Monday 14 April, 1997. This is located in a beautiful and unspoilt part of Sussex near Haywards Heath. It is about 15 miles from the University.

  2. University of London, Royal Holloway College, Egham, Surrey near London. Monday 15 April-Thursday 17 April 1997. This is the annual British Mathematical Colloquium which has low dimensional topology as a special session this year.

Participants may take part in both or either of the above. However participants who wish to take part in the second part must organise their visit through the British Mathematical Colloquium (BMC). The BMC is being organised by Dr G.de Barra, Dept of Maths, Royal Hollaway, University of London, Egham, Surrey, TW20 0EX, Britain. email: g.debarra@rhbnc.ac.uk. More information on the BMC can be found here.

Itinary: participants can arrive at the University of Sussex Conference Centre, Chelwood Gate, independantly if they wish. Otherwise a coach will take participants on Friday evening at 6pm from the University, Mathematics Dept. to the Centre where a meal will be available.

Lectures will take place on Saturday, Sunday and Monday morning. A coach will take participants in the second part on Monday after lunch to the BMC at Egham.

The Tuesday afternoon session will be used as a showpiece of low dimensional topology. The speakers are J.Montesinos, J.Hoste and M.Boileau.

Divertions: there is a comfortable lounge at the centre and the bar will be open in the evenings. It is possible to walk a short distance through the woods to the Red Lion pub. Other walks are possible but since the month is April the weather could be problematic. If there are clear evenings it will be possible to use the telescope and astronomical observatory.

Costs: £229 sterling, this includes conference fee, all meals and accomodation from dinner Friday 11 to lunch Monday 14 April and coach travel from the University. A charge, depending on demand, will be made for transportation to Egham on the Monday after lunch. Please send payments by cheques made out from a British bank or by international money orders.

As mentioned earlier participants who wish to take part in the BMC from Monday pm onwards part must organise their visit through Dr G.de Barra, address above.

Deposit: a refundable deposit of £50 sterling is payable by 1 February, 1997. After then a late booking fee of £30 sterling will be charged.

Diet: Let us know if you have any special dietary requirements.

Funding: a small amount of money has been set aside by the LMS but I would ask participants to find their costs from their own grants, if at all possible. Priority help for accomodation but not travel costs will be given to speakers and British postgraduates.

Transport: the University of Sussex is easily reached by rail or road from London or Gatwick airport or the channel ports. London is most conveniently reached from Heathrow airport by underground train. The conference centre is most easily reached by taxi from Haywards Heath station. The telephone number of the centre is 01825 740283

Participation: If you are interested in this conference please contact Roger Fenn by e-mail: rogerf@solx1.susx.ac.uk. The Centre cannot take many more than 40 residents. So numbers above this will have to be accomodated in nearby guest houses.
