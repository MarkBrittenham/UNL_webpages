<H2> <a name="knots">Knot Theory</a> </H2>

<IMG hspace=20 vspace=20 AlIGN=left SRC= "celt7db.gif" ALT="Picture of a link"> An introduction to <a href="http://www.c3.lanl.gov/mega-math/gloss/knots/knots.html">knot theory</a> which seems to be aimed at teachers of mathematics can be found at <a href="http://www.c3.lanl.gov/">Los Alamos National Laboratory</a>.<p>

There is also another <a href="http://www.cs.ubc.ca/nest/imager/contributions/scharein/KnotSquare.html">knot theory</a> page at the University of British Columbia.<p>

A discussion, and several lists, concerning the classification of knots, may be
found in <a href="http://sgi.ifh.de/~aneziris/contents">Charilaos Aneziris'</a> home page.<p>

This <a href="table9.gif">table</a> of knots up to nine crossings came from <a href="http://www.bioch.ox.ac.uk/~sean/">Sean Collom</a>'s home page at Oxford.<p>

<a href="ftp://chs.cusd.claremont.edu/pub/knot/">ftp://chs.cusd.claremont.edu/pub/knot</a><p>

<a href="gopher://math.berkeley.edu/11/Topology">Berkeley's Topology gopher archive</a> contains, among other things,  Joyce Riley's bibliography of reviews in knot theory(up to about 1990) in Math Reviews.<p>

A <a href="http://www.gang.umass.edu/docs/archive/knots.html">catalog</a> of energy-minimizing configurations of knots, at UMass.<p>
